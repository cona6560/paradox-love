A Tale of Forbidden Love

I found the girl of my dreams

In the most unlikely of places,

What began as a whisper on the wind

Grew into a storm I could not contain.

What was lustful became a prayer

In the stillness of her gaze,

I, enchanted by the scent of her absence,

Trapped in her potion,

She, the balm to a world torn apart by noise.

How I longed to see her face—

To hold her hands like fragile promises,

To breathe in her scent like spring

And so, we met.

We crossed the rivers of time,

Passed through fire and shadow,

Until Friday dawned, and I ascended the stairs

With trembling legs and a heart

Heavy with unnamed fears.

I saw her before me,

A figure draped in black,

My breath caught, my thoughts faltered,

And in that moment,

The world collapsed into a single beat—

A silent quake in the depths of my soul.

She wore a dress that whispered of loneliness,

A red mouth that spoke in unsaid words,

Jewels that weighed heavy on her,

A broken phone in her hands,

As if already planning her escape.

But slowly, like dawn breaking over an unlit horizon,

She smiled,

And the distance between us melted

Into something timeless, something more.

Minutes turned to hours,

And we walked under the stars

With the kind of kiss that sears the heart—

Leaving behind only ashes and dreams.

She had me in a trance,

Her beauty, a question that begged no answer.

Oh, tell me her flaw,

For I was lost in awe.

Her hair, a river I would drown in,

Her waist, a promise I’d never keep.

Her wit, sharper than any sword,

Her laughter, an echo I still hear in my dreams.

Her figure—

A sin I could not repent for.

So tell me her flaw,

For I was lost in awe.

I fell into her like night falls on a barren land,

And soon, I laid bare my soul.

Each passing day unraveled me,

A thread in the hands of an unseen fate.

We were one heart, one pulse,

But the roads beneath our feet began to diverge,

And I—

Ignoring the cracks,

Dreamt only of making her mine.

To live out my days in her shadow,

To see the stars align for us alone.

I would cast away my bloodline,

I would sever the earth, the sky—

Just to be hers,

If only for a moment.

But the days turned to dust,

And all things, even love, must fade.

The world, with its cruel hands,

Tore at the seams of what we built.

The breakups, relentless,

The silences, unbearable—

Yet we clung to the hope

That love could defy the reason of the universe.

But she—

With eyes that had warned me from the start,

Wielded her sword,

And with one final strike,

Severed our ties.

The memory of our passion

Shattered like glass on a cold floor.

I left soon after for distant shores,

To chase another life across the ocean’s edge.

But how can distance mend a soul?

How can new horizons erase what is etched in the heart?

I buried myself in the numbness of the city,

Tried to forget her face,

But her absence echoed in the alleys of my mind.

I filled the void with fleeting comforts,

Drowned myself in shadows of the night,

But no amount of escape could untangle

The knot of regret that held me tight.

Each passing day, a wound that never heals,

Each moment, a reminder of the love I lost.

I can’t bear it anymore,

The weight of her absence,

The silence where her voice once lingered.

I can’t stand it anymore,

This life that spins without meaning.

The only remedy I know—

To see her face again,

To feel her presence beside me,

If only for a fleeting second.

But she is gone, and I remain,

A wanderer lost in my own grief.

She was my forbidden lover,

The one who held my soul in her hands.

How can I live without her?

She was my sunrise, my moonlit night—

And now I walk alone,

Carrying her absence like a scar

Across the heart of my days.
